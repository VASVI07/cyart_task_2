# Priority Justification

## 001 - Phishing Email: Suspicious Link
Priority: High
Reason: Phishing can lead to credential theft or malware delivery through malicious email links.

## 002 - Log4Shell Exploit Detected
Priority: Critical
Reason: Log4Shell is a remote code execution vulnerability with CVSS 9.8 and public exploit availability.

## 003 - Port Scan Detected
Priority: Low
Reason: Port scanning is reconnaissance activity and does not by itself confirm compromise.

## 004 - Brute-force SSH Attempts
Priority: Medium
Reason: Repeated login attempts suggest suspicious activity and possible unauthorized access attempts.

## 005 - Unauthorized Admin Login
Priority: High
Reason: Suspicious privileged account access may indicate account compromise.

## 006 - Ransomware File Detected on Server-X
Priority: Critical
Reason: Ransomware can encrypt files, disrupt systems, and cause major business impact.

## 007 - Suspicious PowerShell Execution
Priority: Medium
Reason: PowerShell can be abused by attackers, but the activity requires validation.

## 008 - Data Exfiltration via Unusual Outbound Traffic
Priority: High
Reason: Unusual outbound traffic may indicate unauthorized transfer of sensitive data.
